01 — Brand Foundation

Who We Are

Blue Lifeline is a water sustainability platform that issues verified Water Benefit Tokens (WBTs) — each representing one cubic meter of certified water benefit. The brand sits at the intersection of environmental impact, blockchain technology, and ESG finance.

🌊

Mission

Enable companies and developers to become credibly water positive by connecting corporate demand to verified, real-world water impact projects.

🎯

Vision

A world where every cubic meter of water use is accountable, measurable, and offset — creating a net positive impact on global water security.

⛓️

Product

Water Benefit Tokens (WBTs) on blockchain, certified under the Volumetric Water Benefit Accounting (VWBA) standard. Measured. Verified. Auditable.

✦

Brand Promise

Credibility above all. Every token is backed by IoT-measured, third-party verified, blockchain-traced water data — no greenwashing possible.

04 — Key Messaging

Brand Language

All language should communicate credibility, precision, and impact. Avoid vague sustainability language — every claim is backed by measurement.

01 Become Water Positive. Credibly. Primary headline / hero CTA
02 The Standard for Water Sustainability Tagline / footer positioning
03 Measured. Verified. Auditable. Product credibility statement
04 Turn Commitments Into Measurable Results Value proposition / closing CTA
05 Offset Water Use With Impact Secondary marketing message

✓ Use

  • Specific, measurable terms (m³, cubic meters, IoT-metered)
  • Credentialed language referencing VWBA certification
  • Active, confident verbs: measure, verify, certify, offset, tokenise
  • Technical precision that signals expertise
  • ESG and sustainability finance terminology
  • Corporate-facing, results-oriented copy

✕ Avoid

  • Vague green language: "eco-friendly", "sustainable future", "going green"
  • Unverified superlatives without data to support them
  • Emotional or sentimental appeals divorced from facts
  • Consumer-facing casual tone
  • Carbon credit analogies without clarification
  • Passive voice or hedged commitments
05 — Logo

Logo Usage

The Blue Lifeline logo consists of the wordmark paired with a droplet/wave icon. It is always reproduced in its approved colour combinations only.

Primary — Dark Background
💧 Blue Lifeline
Reverse — Light Background

Logo Rules

  • Always use the primary version (white wordmark on dark) as the default.
  • A dark-on-light version is available for print or light-background contexts.
  • Maintain clear space of at least 1× the logo height on all sides.
  • Never recolour the logo outside the approved palette.
  • Never stretch, rotate, or apply drop shadows to the logo.
  • Never use the wordmark without its accompanying icon/mark.
  • Minimum digital size: 120px wide. Minimum print size: 25mm wide.
06 — Imagery & Photography

Visual Language

Photography should evoke abundance and scarcity in equal measure — communicating the urgency of the water challenge alongside the tangibility of Blue Lifeline's solutions.

🌊

Water in Scale

Vast bodies of water, ocean horizons, and dramatic cloudscapes. Wide, awe-inspiring shots that communicate the magnitude of the water challenge.

💧

Water Micro-Detail

Water drops on glass, surface texture, ripples and flow. Close-up shots that reinforce precision, measurement, and the scientific nature of verification.

🏗️

Infrastructure & Projects

Sprinklers, water treatment plants, pipelines, and agricultural systems. Photos of real projects that make the impact tangible and credible.

Dark Overlays

All photographic backgrounds use dark overlays or are naturally dark/moody to maintain contrast with white text and the brand's dark aesthetic.

Blue-Cool Tones

Select imagery with cool, blue-leaning colour temperatures. Avoid warm or golden-hour tones — they conflict with the brand's oceanic palette.

Authenticity

Use real project and field photography wherever possible. The brand's credibility story depends on showing genuine infrastructure and impact.

07 — UI Components

Buttons & Controls

All interactive elements use sharp corners (no border-radius), conveying precision and technical authority. Buttons use Syne font, uppercase tracking.

Primary CTA

Electric Water (#00B0FF) fill with navy text. Used for the single most important action on a page — "Contact Us", "Get Started".

Secondary CTA

Outline buttons in white or Electric Water. Used for secondary actions: "Explore Marketplace", "Learn More".

Ghost / Tertiary

Transparent with accent border. Used for developer/partner actions that are important but subordinate to the primary corporate CTA.

08 — Brand Personality

How We Show Up

Authoritative

We speak with the confidence of a VWBA-aligned standards body — not a startup. Our credibility is built on measurement, not marketing.

Technical

We use precise language: m³, IoT meters, blockchain tokenisation, VWBA certification. Technical fluency signals genuine expertise.

Urgent

The global water gap is real and growing. We acknowledge the scale of the problem without catastrophising — we're here with a credible solution.

Transparent

Every claim is backed by data. We don't make promises without verification. Auditability and traceability are core to who we are, not features.
